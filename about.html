Siena Faughnan is a third-generation mixed-media artist, historian, and poet who was raised in Lafayette, Colorado. She grew up in a large family rich with tall tales and seamstresses.

After experiencing early loss, she made beauty and remembrance her lifeâ€™s work, studying history at Colorado College before earning her Masters in Education and teaching high school history. Her work explores themes of grief, faith, and interconnectedness in a wide range of mediums including poetry, non-fiction, collage, printmaking, and soft sculpture. She is particularly interested in popular religion, memory, and our relationship with the divine.

She has studied at the John Campbell Folk School, Penland, MFA Boston, Lighthouse Writers Workshop, UC Berkeley, Anderson Ranch, Textile Arts Center, and the 92nd Street Y. She lives in Brooklyn, New York.
